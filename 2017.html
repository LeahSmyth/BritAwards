2017

The winners of the 2017 BRIT awards were presented with one of five statues designed by the late Zaha Hadid. 

 

Hadid’s vision was, being an architect, to focus efforts more on the 3D element, as the statue had previously been used as a canvas for artists to paint on for the last few years. The design expresses our unwavering optimism for the future and a break from the norm. The biggest challenge was to create something new within certain guidelines, yet achieving a result recognizable to the public.


The family of statues represents diversity and transition. The family of five trophies make up the 2017 BRIT awards statues, a concept designed to represent diversity. “The family is connected by a wave of transition, they are different yet connected,”.
